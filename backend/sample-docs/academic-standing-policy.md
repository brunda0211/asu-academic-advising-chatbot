## Ira A. Fulton Schools of Engineering — Academic Standards

This document outlines the academic standing policies for undergraduate students enrolled in the Fulton Schools of Engineering at Arizona State University.

---

## GPA Requirements

### Good Academic Standing
Students are in good academic standing when they maintain:
- **Cumulative GPA:** 2.00 or higher
- **Major GPA:** 2.00 or higher
- **Semester GPA:** 2.00 or higher

### Dean's List
Students who achieve a semester GPA of 3.50 or higher while enrolled in at least 12 credit hours are placed on the Dean's List. This recognition appears on the official transcript.

---

## Academic Probation

### Conditions for Probation
A student is placed on academic probation when ANY of the following occur:
1. Cumulative GPA falls below 2.00
2. Major GPA falls below 2.00 for two consecutive semesters
3. Semester GPA falls below 1.50

### Probation Requirements
Students on academic probation must:
- Meet with their academic advisor within the first two weeks of the semester
- Develop an Academic Improvement Plan (AIP) with their advisor
- Enroll in no more than 14 credit hours per semester
- Achieve a semester GPA of 2.00 or higher each term while on probation
- Not enroll in more than one new upper-division major course per semester

### Removal from Probation
Probation is removed when the student raises both their cumulative GPA and major GPA to 2.00 or above.

---

## Academic Disqualification

### Conditions for Disqualification
A student may be academically disqualified when:
1. They fail to meet probation requirements for two consecutive semesters
2. Their cumulative GPA falls below 1.50
3. They earn a semester GPA below 1.00

### Disqualification Process
1. Student receives written notification from the Dean's Office
2. Student has 10 business days to appeal the decision
3. Appeals are reviewed by the Academic Standards Committee
4. If disqualified, the student is separated from the university for a minimum of one academic year

### Readmission After Disqualification
Students seeking readmission must:
- Wait a minimum of one academic year (two semesters)
- Submit a readmission application with a personal statement
- Provide evidence of academic readiness (community college coursework, etc.)
- Meet with an academic advisor before re-enrollment
- Follow a prescribed course plan for the first semester back

---

## Grade Replacement Policy

### Course Repeat Rules
- Students may repeat a course in which they earned a grade of D or E
- A course may be repeated a maximum of two times (three total attempts)
- Only the most recent grade is used in GPA calculation
- All attempts remain on the transcript
- Repeated courses count toward the credit hour limit only once

### Grade Forgiveness
- Available to students who have been readmitted after academic disqualification
- Up to 16 credit hours of D and E grades may be excluded from GPA calculation
- Grade forgiveness is a one-time option and is irreversible
- Forgiven courses still appear on the transcript with a notation

---

## Incomplete (I) Grade Policy

### Eligibility
An Incomplete grade may be assigned when:
- The student has completed at least 80% of the coursework
- The student is passing the course at the time of the request
- Circumstances beyond the student's control prevent completion (illness, emergency)

### Completion Timeline
- Students have one calendar year to complete remaining work
- If not completed within one year, the I grade converts to E (failing)
- Extensions may be granted by petition to the instructor and department chair

---

## Academic Integrity

### Violations
Academic integrity violations include:
- Plagiarism (presenting others' work as your own)
- Cheating on exams or assignments
- Unauthorized collaboration
- Fabrication of data or citations
- Facilitating academic dishonesty by others

### Sanctions
Sanctions are progressive and may include:
1. **First offense:** Zero on the assignment + academic integrity education module
2. **Second offense:** Failing grade in the course + notation on transcript
3. **Third offense:** Suspension or expulsion from the university

### Reporting
All suspected violations are reported to the Office of Student Rights and Responsibilities. Students have the right to a hearing and may appeal decisions through the university appeals process.

## Advising Requirements

### Mandatory Advising
The following students must meet with an academic advisor before registering for the next semester:
- Freshmen (fewer than 30 credit hours completed)
- Students on academic probation
- Students who have changed their major within the last semester
- Students with 90+ credit hours who have not filed a graduation application

### Advising Holds
An advising hold prevents registration until the student completes a meeting with their assigned advisor. Holds are placed:
- At the start of each registration period for mandatory advising populations
- When a student's academic plan requires review
- After a student fails a prerequisite course

---

## Withdrawal Policies

### Course Withdrawal
- Withdrawal deadline: End of the 10th week of classes (Fall/Spring)
- Withdrawals after the deadline require a petition with documented extenuating circumstances
- A "W" grade appears on the transcript (does not affect GPA)
- Students may withdraw from a maximum of 3 courses during their undergraduate career without petition

### Complete Withdrawal (All Courses)
- Students withdrawing from all courses must notify the Registrar's Office
- Financial aid implications apply (may require repayment of a portion)
- Students who stop attending without officially withdrawing receive E grades in all courses
